Poutine

Description

Poutine is a delicious Canadian staple.

At it's core, it's a very simple dish consisting of just 3 ingredients: French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

Some popular variations include adding chicken, beef, or pulled pork.

This recipe will show you how to make a traditional poutine. If you've never had it before, I recommend trying it as is before customizing it.

Ingredients

  • 1 quart of vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 (10.25 oz) can of beef gravy
  • 5 medium potatoes, cut into fries
  • 2 cups of cheese curds

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 365 degrees F (185 degrees C).
  2. While the oil is heating, begin to warm the gravy.

  3. Place fries into the hot oil, and cook until light brown (8 to 10 minutes).

    Cook fries in batches if necessary to give them enough room to move a little in the oil.

    Remove the fries from the deep fryer and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain.

  4. Place fries on a serving platter, and sprinkle the cheese curds over them.
  5. Ladle warmed gravy over the fries and cheese. Serve immediately.

Variations

  • Add cut-up cooked chicken or beef.
  • Use shredded mozzarella cheese instead of cheese curds.
  • Italian Poutine: Substitute marinara for gravy.
  • BBQ Poutine: Substitute a light BBQ sauce for gravy.
